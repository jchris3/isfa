Campus life

ISFA is an institute that aims at the integral training of the human being. Beyond the purely academic activities, several other activities are set-up to ensure the full development of our students. Some of these activities are organized by the institute (sports activities, field trips, company visits, participation to the Youth Day, the National Day, etc.) and others by the students themselves (Journalism club, Students unions, students associations, etc…).

Sports activities

Life at ISFA is enhanced by several sports activities (games, sports competitions). In addition to the benefits of sports on health, the objective is to allow intermingling among students and an opening to the outside world.

ISFA usually organizes a mountain-climbing event on Mount Cameroon in Buea. This sporting event that takes place in the first fortnight of March is an opportunity for the participants to discover the “chariot of gods” with its natural riches.

Sports competitions mean that ISFA students can compete with teams of companies or partner organizations and participate in inter-specialty or university games.

Field trips

At ISFA, the adequacy training and employment is a steady concern. This openness to the world of employment is also reflected in the presence of professionals in the teaching staff. From the first year and even before the internships in company, company visits are generally organized to allow our students to become familiar with the working environment. The students then have the opportunity to meet professionals in their posts, to exchange with them and to live the realities of the field.rain.

Participation in the Youth day and the National day

ISFA is a republican institute that does not miss any opportunity to participate in the various events that mark the national life in our country. Thus, ISFA associates itself with all the events and parades organized during the Youth day and the National day.

Creation of clubs

ISFA encourages its students to create and animate different clubs according to their abilities and their interests. These clubs can be of different natures: journalism club, computer club, music club, dance club, etc.

Student Association

The relationships between ISFA and its students are intended to be sustainable. Also, an association gathering new and former students of ISFA is created and facilitated by the students themselves.

This association, which allows them to know one another, to follow their professional development and to keep in touch with various batches, is a factor promoting socio- professional insertion.
